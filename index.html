Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is a classical Indian dance form that originated in the state of Tamil Nadu. It is one of the oldest and most revered dance styles in India, dating back over 2,000 years. The name "Bharatanatyam" is derived from four components: "Bhava" (expression), "Raga" (melody), "Tala" (rhythm), and "Natya" (drama). This dance form is known for its graceful and intricate movements, elaborate footwork, expressive hand gestures (mudras), and emotive facial expressions. Traditionally performed by women, Bharatanatyam narrates mythological stories, religious themes, and historical events through a combination of rhythmic steps and artistic postures.
